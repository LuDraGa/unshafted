# v0.7 Decision-First Results

**Status:** Active next workstream
**Parent roadmap:** `execution-docs/v0.6.7-to-v0.8.0-roadmap.md`
**Scope:** v0.7 product clarity and reuse

## Goal

Make each result answer the user's practical question before showing detailed analysis:

- Can I probably proceed?
- Should I review/clarify first?
- Should I negotiate before signing?
- Should I pause and get legal help?

The result should feel like a signing aid, not a categorized legal report.

## Non-Goals

- No new LLM provider or billing changes.
- No lawyer marketplace or legal handoff flow.
- No full report page outside the popup in this workstream.
- No breaking schema changes for existing local or Drive history.

## Current State

Implemented baseline:

- Quick scan shows a verdict strip, summary, flags, parties, role customization, and detailed-analysis CTA.
- Detailed analysis shows a dark decision banner, top risks, top asks, and detailed accordions.
- Opened saved reports now use the same quick-scan top-level structure as live quick scans.

Remaining gaps:

- Quick scan and detailed analysis still feel like separate result products.
- The decision language is generic and not strongly tied to concrete blockers or asks.
- Evidence is present in detailed cards, but there is no clear path from decision to supporting clause/reference.
- Coverage/confidence signals are scattered rather than attached to the decision.
- Deep-analysis taxonomy still exposes too many overlapping categories.

## Proposed Behavior

### Decision Header

Add one compact decision header for both quick and detailed results:

- Recommendation: `Likely okay`, `Review first`, `Negotiate first`, or `Pause and get help`.
- Rationale: one plain-English sentence explaining why.
- Confidence/coverage: model/source caveat close to the recommendation.
- Reviewed-as role and document type.

### Action Summary

Immediately after the decision header, show:

- `Top blockers` or `Top risks`: 1-3 items that change the signing decision.
- `Ask for this`: 1-3 negotiation asks or clarification questions.

For quick-scan-only results, derive this from red flags and key obligations.
For detailed results, derive this from immediate worries, one-sided clauses, missing protections, timing/lock-in, negotiation ideas, suggested edits, and questions.

### Evidence Path

Keep detailed accordions, but make the path clear:

- `Evidence and clauses` for referenced findings.
- `Other details` for secondary categories.
- `Caveats` for assumptions, extraction warnings, and disclaimer.

Do not hide high-severity findings behind an equal-weight list without a top summary.
